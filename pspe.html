The Program for Semiconductor Product Engineering is an industry sponsored, Bachelors and Master's program to prepare students for professional careers in semiconductor design and manufacturing. Students with bachelor's degrees in electrical engineering can earn a Master of Science in Electrical Engineering degrees. One of the most exciting aspects of this program is that it is a true collaboration between the College of Engineering and many of the leading companies in the semiconductor industry.

The purpose of the program is to help meet the enormous demand for trained semiconductor product engineers. The curriculum is designed to provide students with a working knowledge of both the theory and practice of semiconductor design and manufacturing. Students accepted into this two−year program take course work on the Tech campus for their first two semesters, then participate in an internship at one of the industry sponsors' facilities during the summer and one long semester, and then finish up their degrees with a long semester at Tech.

Features of the Program

  • Provides a broad background in semiconductor technology, including testing, process, design, failure analysis, and business concepts.
  • The program is supported by major companies involved in semiconductor manufacturing and equipment.
  • Industrial internships with semiconductor industries are incorporated into the curriculum.
  • Scholarships for undergraduate and graduate students interested in pursuing careers in the semiconductor industry.

PSPE Seminar

All students in the program must attend the PSPE Seminar, ECE 4120-002, each semester the student recieves a scholarship. PSPE Seminar promotes and develops student's proffesional skills with written, oral, and group activities each week. Students get a number of on-campus and online presentations from sponsoring industry partners, giving student's a competitive edge and insight into real experiences in industry.

Requirements

Students in the program may come from a variety of undergraduate backgrounds in science and engineering with minimal leveling. Undergraduate and Graduate students with good academic records are invited to apply for the program.

Students interested in the program with the necessary prerequisites should apply to the program or contact us to learn more.

Academic Prerequisites

  • Mathematics through differential equations
  • Chemistry
  • Two semesters of calculus-based physics
  • A minimum undergrad GPA of 3.0 (4.0 scale)

Students interested in applying should be prepared to submit various application materials.

Application Materials

  • Professional Resume
  • Cover Letter
  • Graduate Record Examination (GRE) Score (Grad Only)
  • English Language Proficiency Score (Foreign Transfer Only)

These application materials will meet Texas Tech University's Graduate Admissions Requirements. This includes minimum English Language Proficiency Scores and Graduate Record Examination Scores.
